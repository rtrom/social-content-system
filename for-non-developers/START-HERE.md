# Your Content Helper for Claude — Start Here

This adds a set of **skills** to your Claude so it can help you plan and write
social media content (blog posts, TikTok, Reels, Shorts, X, Threads, newsletters)
that all sound like *you*.

You do this setup **once**. It takes about 15 minutes. You will not need to touch
any code or files — just click and type. No GitHub account or login needed.

---

## Part 0 — Download the skills (30 seconds)

Click this link — it downloads one zip file, no login required:

**https://github.com/rtrom/social-content-system/raw/master/for-non-developers/social-content-skills-handoff.zip**

Then **unzip it** (right-click → Extract / double-click on Mac). You'll get this
guide plus 17 small skill zip files. Keep that folder open — you'll use those zips
in Part 2.

---

## Part 1 — Turn on the setting Claude needs (1 minute)

1. Open Claude in your browser (**claude.ai**) or the Claude desktop app.
2. Click your **name/initials** (bottom-left) → **Settings**.
3. Go to **Capabilities**.
4. Find **"Code execution and file creation"** and switch it **ON**.

That's it — this is what lets the skills actually run.

---

## Part 2 — Add the skills (about 12 minutes, one time)

You were given a folder of **17 small zip files** (one per skill). You'll add each
one. It's the same 4 clicks every time.

For **each** zip file:

1. In Claude, go to **Settings → Customize → Skills**.
2. Click the **" + "** button.
3. Choose **"+ Create skill"**, then **"Upload a skill."**
4. Pick one of the zip files. Done — it appears in your list.

Repeat until all 17 are added. **Tip:** start with **`brand-voice.zip`** — it's the
important one everything else builds on.

> If a zip ever won't upload, just skip it and come back to it — the others still work.

### The 17 skills you're adding
- **brand-voice** ← set this up first (see Part 3)
- blog-outline, content-brief, content-calendar, repurposing
- tiktok-script, tiktok-caption
- reels-script, reels-caption
- shorts-script, shorts-caption
- x-copy, threads-copy, newsletter-copy
- trend-research, niche-evaluator, creator-growth-patterns

---

## Part 3 — Teach Claude your voice (5 minutes, one time)

Before writing anything, set your voice. In a new chat, just type:

> **"Help me set up my brand voice."**

Claude will ask you a handful of questions (who you're talking to, how you want to
sound, words to avoid). Answer them honestly. When it's done, it saves a short
**brand voice profile** — and every other skill uses it automatically so your
content stays consistent.

**Keep your voice for later:** so you don't have to redo this every time, the easy
way is to do your content work inside a Claude **Project**:
1. Left sidebar → **Projects → New project** (call it "Content").
2. When Claude shows you your finished brand voice profile, copy it and paste it into
   the project's **instructions/knowledge** box.
3. From then on, start your content chats *inside that project* — Claude already
   knows your voice every time.

---

## Part 4 — Use it (this is the whole point)

You never need commands or menus. Just talk to Claude in plain English. Examples:

- *"Should I make content about \[your idea]? Evaluate the niche for me."*
- *"Find what's trending in \[your topic]."*
- *"Write me a full content set about \[topic]"* → you get a blog post plus matching
  TikTok, Reels, Shorts, X, Threads, and newsletter versions, all from the one story.
- *"Turn this blog post into a week of social posts."*

Claude picks the right skill on its own. If you ever want it to use a specific one,
just name it: *"Use the reels-script skill for this."*

---

## If something seems off

- **Content sounds generic or not like you?** Re-run *"Help me set up my brand voice"*
  and update your answers — everything picks up the change.
- **Claude isn't using the skills?** Double-check Part 1 (the "Code execution and file
  creation" setting) is still ON.
- **Need the trend-data tools?** Those are optional and separate — ignore them to
  start; everything here works without them.

That's everything. Set your brand voice once, then just ask for what you want.
