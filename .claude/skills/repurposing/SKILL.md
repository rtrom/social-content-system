# Repurposing

Turns the finished **pillar** post into every channel's **derivative**. A derivative is not a fresh take and not a copy-paste — it is the pillar's message re-shaped for one channel's native form. This skill is the shared method every channel skill applies; the channel skills add per-platform format on top of it.

The rule that makes the system coherent: **every channel derives from the pillar, never from raw research.** Eight writers researching separately produce eight contradicting stories. One pillar, eight derivations, produces one story eight ways. If a channel writer reaches back past the pillar to re-research, coherence is already broken.

## The four moves

Every derivative is built from these, chosen to fit the channel. This is a flat set — a given channel uses whichever moves its form needs:

1. **Pull** — lift an exact quote, stat, or line from the pillar and let it stand as the derivative's spine (a tweet built on one sharp pillar sentence; a caption quoting a pillar line). The pulled material is verbatim from the pillar, so it cannot contradict it.

2. **Restructure (hook-first)** — reorder the pillar's logic so the payoff leads. Video and social invert the pillar's build-up: the conclusion or the sharpest point goes first as the hook, then the support follows. Same content, front-loaded.

3. **Compress** — reduce the pillar to its core message plus one or two key points for short formats. Compression drops points; it never adds or alters them. What survives is a true subset of the pillar.

4. **Expand** — for the newsletter, take the pillar's frame and add the personal/contextual layer a longer-form email affords — without introducing claims the pillar and brief do not contain. Expansion adds *texture*, not new *claims*.

## Applying it

For any channel:

1. **Anchor to pillar and brief.** Open the finished pillar and the `content-brief` anchor. The core message and CTA are fixed by the brief; do not restate them differently. Completion: pillar and brief both loaded before drafting.

2. **Choose the moves this channel needs.** A tweet: pull + compress. A video script: restructure + compress. A newsletter: expand. Completion: the moves are chosen to fit the channel's native form, not applied mechanically.

3. **Derive, checking against the pillar.** Produce the draft. Every claim in it must trace to the pillar or brief — nothing invented, nothing contradicting. Completion: each factual claim in the derivative is traceable to a specific pillar passage or brief point. A claim with no pillar source is the failure this skill exists to prevent — cut it or take it back to the brief.

## Note on the QA pass

The final `editor-qa` review checks all derivatives against each other and the pillar for cross-channel contradiction. Clean derivation here is what makes that pass find nothing — the two work together.
