# YouTube Shorts — Divergences from TikTok

Disclosed reference for `shorts-script`. Only what differs; the shared short-form mechanics carry over.

## Discovery model

- Shorts surface heavily via **search and suggested** on YouTube, not just a pure FYP feed. This makes the spoken keyword and the topic clarity matter more — say the thing the viewer searched.
- Viewers often arrive from a channel they know; a Short can assume marginally more context than a cold TikTok.

## Loop and length

- Shorts loop hard — a script that ends where it began (callback to the hook) rewards the loop.
- Up to 60s; the same one-point compression applies. Sub-30s still performs best for a single point.

## Audio

- YouTube's audio library and trending sounds are its own; source trend audio from the YouTube signal, not TikTok or Reels.
- Original narration is strong on Shorts given the search-driven, informational lean.

## Register

- Slightly more informational/utility-leaning than TikTok's entertainment default, reflecting search intent — but native register still beats polish.

## What does NOT change

- Hook-first, beat spine, one idea per beat, muted-viewing on-screen text. Shared method — do not re-derive.
