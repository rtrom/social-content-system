# Reels Caption — IG Conventions

Disclosed reference for `reels-caption`. Only what differs from TikTok caption norms.

## Caption length

- Instagram tolerates and rewards a longer caption than TikTok — up to a short paragraph. The caption can carry a real beat of the message (a pulled pillar line plus a line of context), not just a one-line frame.
- Front-load the first sentence — it shows before "…more" in the feed.
- A light line-break/whitespace structure reads better on IG than a wall of text.

## Hashtags

- 3–8 tags; Instagram's practical reward tops out well below the old 30-tag maximum. Do not stuff.
- Mix: broad reach + niche + one trend/keyword tag sourced from the Instagram trend signal (not the TikTok one).
- Placing tags at the end of the caption (or first comment) both work; keep them out of the reading line.

## What does NOT change

- The CTA comes from the brief; the caption complements the video rather than transcribing it. Same as TikTok.
