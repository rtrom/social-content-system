# TikTok Caption — Conventions

Disclosed reference for `tiktok-caption`.

## Caption

- Short — one to two lines. The caption is not where the content lives (the video is); it frames or adds a beat the video doesn't.
- Can pose the open loop the video closes, or add a punchline the video sets up.
- First few words matter most — they show before "…more".
- CTA in caption is fine but soft; hard CTAs live in the video button.

## Hashtags

- 3–5 total. TikTok does not reward hashtag stuffing.
- Mix: one broad/reach tag, one to two niche tags, and one trend tag from the `trend-research` signal where a live trend applies.
- Avoid banned/shadow-flagged generic tags (#fyp alone does little).

## On-screen-text callouts

- The punch overlays for the editor — the 2–4 text moments that should hit on screen (the hook line, a key stat, the turn).
- Distinct from the script's running on-screen text (which tracks the spoken words); these are the emphasis beats.
